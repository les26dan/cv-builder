# CV Builder Product Roadmap

## Solution Ideation & Craft

MVP 1.01

### Big Hairy Question

"Why is this 10x better than what people are already doing or using?"

### OKR

#### Goals

* Launch a usable MVP that proves real user behavior (not hypothetical interest).  
* Demonstrate that users not only try but return, activate, and use the product meaningfully.

#### Key Metrics

* Solution appeal: Net Promoter Score (NPS) \> 40  
* “where has this been” sentiment \>40% || Sean Ellis’ “40% would be very disappointed” test   
* 20–30% of users express willingness to pay in interviews/surveys  
* 15–25% conversion on waitlist page with pricing visible  
* ≥15% of pre-launch users submit real pre-payment details or refundable deposits (ideal)"

## MVP Testing

MOAT 2.01

### OKR

#### Goals

* Launch a usable MVP that proves real user behavior (not hypothetical interest).  
* Demonstrate that users not only try but return, activate, and use the product meaningfully.

#### Key Metrics

* "Activation rate \>40%  
* Retention:  
  * D1: 30-40%  
  * D7 \>25%  
  * D30: 5-10%  
  * DAU/WAU ratio \>0.3

Willingness to pay:  
Soft Success Metrics (Signs of Momentum):  
        •        10% of activated users express intent to upgrade (not just use)  
        •        15% trial-to-paid conversion among first cohort  
        •        25% clickthrough rate on upgrade or pricing pages  
        •        Users engage pricing content (compare plans, explore limits)

Hard Success Metrics (Signals of Real Revenue Traction):  
        •        Trial-to-paid conversion ≥15–20% (or higher depending on model)  
        •        Paying user activation rate ≥40% (they are not just paying—they’re using)  
        •        User willingness to pay ≥ pre-defined price anchor (e.g. ≥$10/mo)  
        •        \<60-day payback period on early paid acquisition (if testing ads at MVP level)"
